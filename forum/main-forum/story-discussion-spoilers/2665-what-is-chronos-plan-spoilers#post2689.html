    What is Chronos' plan? [SPOILERS]

    03-20-2018, 11:31 PM
    I think he planning on betraying The Door. I will assume The Door is Alvin.

    It's illogical for Chronos to give Ignis immunity to time freezing as Ignis is trouble. The Door must have given Ignis the immunity to counteract Chronos and us. This implies that The Door doesn't trust Chronos or us.

    Ezra has stated that Chronos' plan will definitely destroy Alynica. From Chronos' actions: freezing the meteors, refusing to fight Ezra even if he kills Riley: Ezra's statement is questionable. Ezra knows that Chronos is working for The Door. I infer that this means that The Door's plan will destroy Alynica.

    The Door probably concluded that Chronos is the biggest risk to his plan before Ezra was unfrozen. Chronos is very untrustworthy. Alvin must have focused all his resources on making sure Chronos doesn't backstab him. But than Chronos unleashed a very dangerous wildcard: Ezra. Alvin must now concentrate on preventing Ezra from ruining his plan. This leaves space for Chronos to do something while Alvin is distracted. Alvin realized that Chronos unfroze Ezra, which makes him suspect that Chronos is a traitor. Chronos will probably needs his full power back.

    Why allow Ignis to kill Copper mages and burn down Timecrest? Alvin may have ordered those killings as a threat to Chronos. If you backstab me, I will make sure you pay. It's illogical that Chronos will suggest that Ignis kill mages from his guild as these mages help fund his guild by carrying out missions. At the Timecrest fire, if you go to Ash's block, you find out that Timecrest is testing replacing wolfdogs with toads as they're cheaper. Something appears to be draining Chronos' wealth.

    He may have build the bomb at the Nightshade Forest. Why?

    Ignis and his underlings know way too much about Ash. Chronos may be giving The Door information on Ash but how much of that information is true? At the Timecrest Fire, Ignis blahs on about how much Chronos will despise Ash as Chronos is completely ruthless. Chapter 10 (Trust Chronos), Chronos will say something out of character, it's in character that he is frustrated that Ash hates him but very out of character for Chronos to actually say it. He may be putting on an act to trick Alvin and Ignis. Ignis appears to think that Chronos is faking.

    Alvin may know that Chronos destroyed Valdor because King Janus murdered his parents or not. It depends on who sent Ash back in time to view Chronos' past. Or whether Alvin was there on the day of Valdor's destruction.

    We know Chronos' true goal. He wants to see his loved ones alive and happy. When he gets that, why not finish off Alvin and Ignis? Ignis wants to kill Ash. Ignis' father seems to want to murder Chronos' mother. Alvin's plan will result in Alynica's end. His loved ones literally live in Alynica. He benefits in eliminating Ignis and Alvin.

      OK. I read up on the psychology of evil and Ezra's memories.

      Mass murderers tend to have three traits: authoritarianism, ideological commitment and external lost of control. I don't believe that Chronos respects authority seeing as he tortures King Janus. Chronos is extremely committed to his family which means he will stick to his goal no matter what. It doesn't matter if innocents get killed, Ash gets hurt, his own guild is burnt down, or Alynica is destroyed as long as Chronos gets his family alive again.

      His parents dying when he was 13 fills the "external lost of control". I think his father's lesson on things that you can't change, shouldn't change backfired. When baby Ash went missing, young Chronos probably went I can't change the truth that my sibling is gone and gave up. In the game, a bunch of bad things happen because Chronos lost control of the situation. Examples: Ignis nearly killed Ash, Ezra killed Riley, Ignis killed A.C.

      Chronos is odd because he's a lone wolf. He alone destroyed Valdor. In the human world, lone wolfs are extremely rare and extremely hard to predict.
