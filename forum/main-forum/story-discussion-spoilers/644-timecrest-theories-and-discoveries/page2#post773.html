            Hello all, sorry that I haven't posted in a while. College keeps me busy. But I found something very interesting when re-exploring Timecrest 1. I wanted to have a complete run as a good base to go through Timecrest 2 with, but with Gravitas Magnus instead of Levitas Magnus. However, I found someone I have never seen before. Be warned, this may contain spoilers even for people who have played through the game several times. This was new to me and I have completed well over 100 runs. And this character raised a big question with me. WHO IS THE BEING OF LIGHT??? After trying to do everything I could in the first part of the game, I discovered a scene I had never seen before atop Mount Asura. First, let me list all of the things I accomplished beforehand.
            I learned Ash is a descendent of humans.
            I met the mysterious Dragon Rider.
            I learned that the Dragon Rider's name is Ezra.
            I saw Riley with Ash's Parents in Lyall.
            I witnessed the Valdorian disease in Valdor.
            I encountered the flower girl in Valdor.
            Lyall was destroyed, killing Ash's parents.
            Ash killed King Janus Valdor, Riley's Father.
            Riley wrote Ash a letter to warn of danger.
            Ash learned that the hooded man is Guildmaster Fulgur.
            In addition, the first message in the red text didn't come when the meteors fell on Mount Asura, but while Lyall was being destroyed... Already I could tell something was off. Yet what to come far outweighs this scene.
            Following all of those achievements, I saw an intriguing scene when I reached Asura. The first thing I saw was an illusion of Guildmaster Petora. She said "I see. This was that old bastard's doing." Following that, Ash asks what she wants. And Petora responds "I want you to leave Mount Asura, Ash, and come with me. Everything is not as it seems."
            Already this seemed quite odd from the other hundred (literally, at least) times I have completed this. Then Ash continued upwards, and found a family of muffins that gave me a piece of Irillium. Now I knew that something was very different. I had never encountered a family of muffies. Not in any run, even when I climbed the same path, never had I seen that. However, next piece of dialogue was what shocked me the most.Ash begins to remember things. There was a person who was radiating brilliant light with their back to him. Seven glyphs of different colors are circling him. And then, across from the Being of Light is none other than Young Chronos! The Being of Light's attack threw Chronos up the mountain, and that was the end of the vision. And Ash is as shocked by the scene as we are.
            Then when Ash follows where Young Chronos was thrown, we reach another platform. Ash doesn't see Young Chronos or the Being of Light, but does see a young girl. She has blood trickling down her face and cuts and bruises all over her body. And her hair keeps changing colors. Yes, this small girl is Young Guildmaster Petora! The only thing she says is "Luthor... don't... you don't have to..." And then she disappears.
            Going up once more, we find Young Chronos and the Being of Light once more! The Being of Light is holding Young Chronos up by the throat while his feet dangle in the air. While all seems lost for him, he chants a spell that forces the Being of Light to drop Young Chronos. And then they both disappear.
            Needless to say, this was by far the biggest discovery I had ever come upon. I have not yet found anything pertaining to the mysterious Being of Light as of yet in Timecrest 2, but I haven't gotten too far in the story yet. I am currently in Nightshade Forest. However, it does show up when looking at the list of achievements, so I have to think there is going to be some hint into who this mysterious Being of Light is in Timecrest 2.             Who do you think this mysterious character is? How does he fit into the story? Have you ever encountered him?
            Sashimioishi Also, I wanted to mention that you did an excellent job with your theory, it covered a ton of what I would like to cover in my Ezra theory. I hope you don't mind if I quote your stuff a little bit! Haha! From one theorizer to another, excellent job!!!

                It has been quite a while since I last posted. Work has kept me exceptionally busy, but never busy enough to not read all the wonderful theories you guys have written!!!
                I have 2 new theories, but I will only cover 1 of them in this post.
                Where did the time crystals come from? By definition, they are "Mysterious dark crystals that emit a pale blue light." As we know, they are used to move forward in time. I believe that these crystals were created by Young Chronos. During Ash's flashbacks, when baby Ash and young Chronos are in his lab, Chronos is "casting magic over some crystals." Perhaps these were the earliest experiments of Chronos's capabilities with time magic? Whether early or late, I believe it is quite clear that these crystals were the results of Chronos's experimentation. But one interesting thing to note is the pale blue light is that it is the same description of the look of the Alycnian glyphs that appear on Ash's pocket watch. Though it would be a long shot with little supporting evidence, perhaps the blue light has something to do with time magic and Chronos also cast a spell over the pocket watch after its creation that allowed his human companion to cast magic in their world. If humans are unable to cast magic in Alycnia (with the player as the obvious exception), perhaps the pocket watch has something to do with it as a gateway not only to communication with the human world, but magic as well? As in inventor, Chronos would work tirelessly to make his pocket watch the best that it could be and beyond the function of regular communication. It would not be the most farfetched idea to think that Chronos made the pocket watch capable of using Time Magic in Alyncia as part of his experimentation with the device. Also it could be an interesting thing to note about the Timecrest Merchant's great interest in the Time Crystals, so much that he would sell his greatest invention for them. Maybe he figured out that Chronos created the crystals, or that he is trying to discover how to use them. The crystals would be invaluable to anyone in Alyncia, as they would be able to manipulate time themselves. Maybe the Timecrest Merchant is trying to use them to control time in some way as well.
