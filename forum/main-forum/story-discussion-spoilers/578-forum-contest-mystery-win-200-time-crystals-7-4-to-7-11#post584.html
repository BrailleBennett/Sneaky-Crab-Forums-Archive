    Forum Contest: Mystery. WIN 200 TIME CRYSTALS! (7/4 to 7/11)

    07-04-2016, 03:09 PM
    The contest is closed!

    The winner of this contest is Ben who by far had the most elaborate and interesting theories, winning 200 Time Crystals! To be clear, this doesn't confirm or deny Ben's theories correct about Timecrest, just that it was the most well written and interesting analysis.

    We chose another person at random, and this is HealerLii, winning 100 Time Crystals!

    We greatly enjoyed reading your theories, some were spot on, and some were completely off. But you are all asking the right questions, and luckily nobody asked anything that we didn't already know the answers to. (No plot holes yet, phew!)

    I'll be PMing the winners shortly with instructions on how to receive their prizes!

    -----

    Hello Timecresters and welcome to our ninth forum contest!

    Get your chance to win some free Time Crystals!

    This week's event will again be giving the best answer to the question below for a chance to win 200 Time Crystals!
    There will also be a participant randomly selected at the end of the event winning 100 Time Crystals!

    Rules and Information:
    • You may post multiple times but your entry will be only counted once.
    • Members are encouraged but not required to respond to other member's posts.
    • Posts must be relevant to the event or will not be counted as an entry.
    • Try to give the most thoughtful answer possible for the best chance to win.
    • We will direct PM your account if you are selected as a winner by the end of the contest.
    • Contest will end on July 11th, 2016 at midnight PDT.
    • Posts below may contain spoilers. Please use [spoiler][/spoiler] tags if you are spoiling any major plot points in Timecrest 2.
    To enter, please answer the following question:
    1. What mysteries or questions in Timecrest 1 or 2 do you most want to know the answers?
    2. What is your best theory as to what the answers will be?
    Good luck to all entries!

      Warning, lots of spoilers. Be sure to have completed Timecrest 2 before viewing.


      Who is The Good Man? Is "The Door" an inspiration for his poems?
      Personally, I believe The Good Man is Ash's father. He must have accomplished extraordinary things to have been the architect behind Valdor's Royal Palace.

      Have A.C/Riley been revived? If so, has their revival cursed others?
      I do believe that A.C/Riley has been revived, and though I would not be surprised if it had cursed others, I would also not be surprised if it was not the case. The only instance of revival magic that we know of was from the old story behind the Rhinehart Empire. Ignis clearly found a way and there is a good chance that his revival magic is different than that used in the days of the Rhinehart Empire.

      How was Scarlet Ignis revived? Did Ignis's revival curse Riley and Valdor as theorized in Timecrest 2?
      I believe that though it may have drove him mad, Ignis found another form of revival magic out of his anger for Scarlet's murder. That would also explain why he had been driven mad. I don't believe that Riley would be the one cursed, as we did see firsthand in Timecrest 1 how the Valdor empire was destroyed and found the reasons behind it in Chapter 10. There is too many coincidences for it to just be a curse that drove Chronos to such destruction.

      Where does the door lead? Is it heaven? Could it be a world where people can be revived?
      I don't know where the door leads, however my best guess would be to a place either in the past or in a null-time dimension, much like Ash's pouch. Chronos could have saved his parents and placed them behind the door.

      Just a thought, but could the blue and red text that appear when Ash is dreaming be his mother and father speaking to him behind the door?
      I believe so. First and foremost, the first features of the door show the peculiar red text in the odd font in the promo and in the end of Chapter 5. Focusing on the promo more, the red text was the real feature, and what it said is never truly evil, though it can be interpreted as such. So, if one of Ash's parents would be the red voice, perhaps the blue voice could be the other parent. In the end of Chapter 10, Ash looks through the keyhole and sees both of his parents, and since both voices use the same font but different colors, it would stand to reason that the two voices are Ash's 2 parents.

      Who was the previous connecter to the human world?
      Personally, my belief is that it was Ash's father. Going back to my previous answer, the blue text speaks a friendly voice, and I believe would be the previous connecter. The reason I believe that the blue text would resemble Ash's father rather than his mother is that Ash's father has shown extraordinary accomplishments, though the only one we know of is the architecture of the Valdorian Royal Palace. In addition, Chronos's text is the same blue color, making me associate it with masculinity. Therefore, through logic and deductive reasoning, we can assume that the previous connector and the blue text are Ash's father.

      If the pocket watch is related to the human connection, where did the pocket watch come from?
      The pocket watch was most likely a relic from the human world. My guess was, because it was passed down in Ash's adoptive family, it may have been passed from a human relative or from the time where Ash's adoptive ancestors were friendly with humans.

      If Chronos is human, how did he develop his powers so well?
      A lot was mentioned about Chronos's experiments, though it happened only once in Timecrest 1, with Ash's null-time pouch, it can be implied from what Riley said that Chronos conducts a lot of these experiments. In Chapter 10, we are given evidence that he has been doing these experiments from a young age, which is also where Ash got his scar on his hand. In that text, Chronos mentioned that he could only turn back time 5 minutes, which makes me think that he worked through experimentation to build his exceptional skills over a large number of years.

      What were Chronos's experiments at the end of Timecrest 2?
      I believe that Chronos's experiments may have had to do with time magic as well as practicing control of other substances, perhaps like control of the acidic chemical that got onto Ash's hand. It seemed like he had a whole lab to himself which makes me think that he worked constantly on these experiments.

      What did Chronos do to Ezra to have Ezra form such a grudge against him?
      I believe that Ezra may have hated Chronos because of his ability to gain such powers as a human descendant. From the evidence presented, Chronos and Ezra don't seem to be much different in a few regards. They are both self-taught, they both seem to want to be alone, and they both have a strong passion for their beliefs, whatever they may be. Though I don't know the specific thing that Chronos may have done to Ezra, my guess would have to be that he learned his magic through extreme experimentation rather than trances, as Ezra may seem to believe magic has to be taught.

      Why does Ezra seem to care so much about Ash and his opinions? Is it to get closer to Chronos, as Petora theorized in Chapter 6?
      I believe Ezra cares about Ash because of the passion and commitment to his beliefs. Ezra is also very passionate, and he does say in Chapter 3, "I [see] myself in you." I think that what Petora said may have some truth to it, however I believe that he may be studying Ash's psyche to see if he can learn anything about how to defeat Chronos from it.

      What is Chronos's control over the guild masters?
      I think Chronos does not have the guild masters under his will per say, but I think that he, as the leader that the guild was named after, they have great respect for him and follow what he says out of extreme respect.

      Did Chronos destroy Valdor because of its oppressive power or the scene at the end of Chapter 10 where King Janus Valdor kicks apart his parent's hands in their dying attempt to be close to one another?
      I believe that Chronos destroyed Valdor because of how King Janus kept torturing his parents and kicked their hands apart when after he killed them. Hell, I don't blame him for everything that he did anymore. If I were him, and in his position of power, I may have done the same.

      What other human technology has been concealed in Alcynia?
      I believe that much of our technology, though versions behind, exists in Alcynia, hence the laptop that Chronos has in Chapter 8 and the bomb that Team 13 finds later while exploring the forest. I am also sure that entire human cities are hidden there, though like mentioned in Timecrest 2, they are hidden under heavy magic, which I could only imagine to be like a forcefield.

      What was the original connection that the human world and Alcynia had? Were they like pen pals or was there a worm hole of sorts between worlds?
      I believe that the original connection would have been much more like a worm hole than just pen pals. Lots of human technology and from the context in Timecrest 2 an entire human civilization existed with Alcynia. I wouldn't be surprised if our worlds were connected at a time but after the destruction of Valdor, much of the connection was severed.

      Is Ash completely human?
      I know that not everyone may have picked this up, but in one of the flashbacks in Chapter 10, Ash's parents mentioned what I interpreted to mean that Ash is a living half-human half-Alcynian. This would make more sense with Ash's father being the architect behind Valdor's Royal Palace and Ash and Chronos's inherent ability to cast magic, where I assume all humans may not to be able to achieve such feats.

            There are a lot of things I'm curious about after reading Timecrest 2:

            Who is sending us those red messages?
            I suspect it could be Chronos who is sending us those messages. We received a message at the beginning of Chapter 8 I believe, that was in the same font as the the red messages, but in the same color as the texts we get from Chronos. Perhaps this is a hint that those messages were coming from him all along.

            Why are people ordered not to kill Ash?
            For this one, I suspect, Ash being someone we can bring back with our time magic somehow makes her special. Maybe Ash actually has powers that no one else in the world has because of her connection to us. That's why so many powerful people are interested in Ash and don't want to kill her without understanding her true potential.

            Why are the guildmasters following Chronos?
            Petora seems like she loves Chronos, but that leaves Olliver Crane and Fulgur Bray. Olliver seems pretty independent and I'm guessing he follows Chronos because he might be a childhood friend of his. Fulgur is the wildcard. He seems crazy and uncontrollable at times and that leaves me to believe that there is some exchange going on between Chronos and Fulgur where they promised to work with each other for some ultimate goal whatever that may be.

            Then there's Ezra. Everything about the First Mage is a curious mystery. Why hasn't he attacked Chronos yet? How did he lose to Chronos in his battle on top of Mount Asura originally? Why did his rule of Valdor get deleted from the history tomes?
            There must be something that's preventing Ezra from attacking Chronos. What's most interesting to me is that I have a feeling that Ezra could probably defeat Chronos if he wanted to, but when I met Ezra on Mount Asura, he said it wasn't about Chronos. I have a feeling that whatever the reason Ezra isn't fighting Chronos right now is the same reason he lost to Chronos originally and that it doesn't have anything to do with his power level.

            What is the relationship between Ignis and Chronos and why does Ignis seem to hate Ash for some reason?
            Ignis really seemed like he wanted to kill Ash in the end even though we know there are orders not to kill Ash. Also Chronos mentioned that Ignis was going to let pettiness get in the way of his orders. All we know is that Ignis rescued Ash from the cave spider, but that shouldn't make him see Ash as his mortal enemy. So I'm curious in learning more about Ignis' backstory.
