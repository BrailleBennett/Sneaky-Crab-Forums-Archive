    Weekly Question: Who is the Blue Text? Open to 2-27-2017!

    02-20-2017, 10:57 AM
    We talk a lot about the sinister and poetic Red Text. But what about the Blue Text? An old friend? A mysterious hero? Who could this message be from? Our past? The past users of the Nexutas spell? Who is our mysterious ally, and what role will they play in the future? As with every question, the best theory will get 300 Time Crystals, if we get 5 posts we will have a second place winner of 100 Time Crystals, and if we get 8 posts, we will give a prize out to a lucky participant!!! So without further ado, fire away!!!

    I had a plan for this, but I'm unable to use said plan, as I'm halfway through Chapter 6 right now, so I'll have to definitely use the rest of the week to rush to Chapter 8 just for this theory.

    However in the meantime, I'm still able to write this theory.

    Theres only been three people we've met who's been able to send a message through this pocket watch. Ash, Chronos, and the mysterious red text. Obviously the person sending the mysterious red text wouldn't be our friendly blue text, because not only is it not handwritten, or in red, but it isn't a creepy message or poetic message like we normally receive from them.
    Ash is clearly not the mysterious person either, because Ash always has white text, indicating nothing special.

    This leaves us with one person, Chronos. Immediately you'd think this due to the text colour, but we have more proof.
    If you go to Windsorhowl with Roger, Riley will follow you to Wispy Brook instead of Team 13, where she will talk about multiple topics, one of which being is our connection to Ash, and how it reminded her of another connection. The connection between Luthor and his human friend, another thing that is mentioned, related to this topic, is how the pocked watch Ash uses to communicate with us is the very same one Luthor used to communicate to his friend.

    (This is just a first draft, there's more to come, but I just wanna have this post followed, and it's easier to have a hollow draft than a full brainstorm with no way to know how to write it down)
