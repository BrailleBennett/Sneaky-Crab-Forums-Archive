    Weekly Question: Why was Ash given up for adoption?

    04-24-2017, 10:53 AM
    We all know that Ash is a descendent of humans. We all know that Ash was adopted by the Eldens when he was only an infant. We all know that it was Riley who delivered Ash to them in secret. If you look at the amount of time you go back in Chapter 3, we can observe that it is 16 years, which is how old Ash is, making Ash not even 1 when he was adopted. We have all these facts, but one burning question remains. Why was Ash given up by the Timecrests? In the flashbacks, they appeared to be a pretty happy family, so what is the missing link between the flashbacks and Chapter 3? And who exactly decided that he be given up? I mention this because of the scene in Chapter 10 where we see the dead bodies of Helena and Julius, which at that time would make Chronos the only guardian for Ash. So, who gave up baby Ash and why? As with every weekly question, the best theory receives 300 Time Crystals, if we have 5 theories, we will have a second prize of 100 Time Crystals, and if we have 8 theories, we will have a random prize of 50 Time Crystals. So, without further ado, fire away!

      I don't believe Ash was given up by the Timecrests- as you stated, Helena and Julius were no longer around to care for Ash, and Chronos was still a child himself. Chronos has no idea what happened to baby Ash, only realizing Ash was still alive with the first time change. So what happened?

      I believe Ash was kidnapped by Riley. In chapter 3, Ash states that she would never be allowed to leave without guards or royal clothing, so she snuck out with an unknown bundle. She had to get Ash away from people that would cause (him) harm, and what better place than a tiny farming village? She knew the Eldens and trusted them. No one would be suspicious of she ever visited again- they were used to her visiting flowers there. But why?


      As previously stated, Chronos was too young to care for Ash. We knew he already caused Janus a good amount of trouble, based on the guards reactions before they faced off. The nicest possibility was that Riley knew that her father was ready to exterminate all humans, and what better person to kill than Chronos' sibling? But a much worse possibility- we also know Janus would be willing to experiment on an infant, perhaps looking for the root of this time magic, finding what (heh heh) makes Ash tick. Riley was not kidnapping maliciously- she hoped (he) would be raised with a normal life, far away from the glamour of magic. She was simply looking for a way to keep baby Ash away from the carnage.

      Riley was shocked to see Ash again in Timecrest guild. She was afraid to allow Ash to see Chronos about the meteors, as she didn't realize he learned Ash was still alive with the first time shift, and she wanted to warn him first. She may have been afraid of Chronos recruiting Ash for whatever would happen between him and Ezra. Everything she did in the past was to save him.

      Now the question I wonder (which will hopefully be answered in part 3), is what happened to young Chronos from the last flashback to the fight with Janus? He had no parents, no loving adoptive Eldens, to raise him or train him. And yet he still became powerful enough to stop the meteors and build Aion. Perhaps the warning about trusting strangers comes from personal experience. Yet he admits he wishes that he learned that the way Ash did.

        Ash was adopted in 998, because his birth parents had died from the Valdorian disease, which they probably caught while visiting the king. During that visit, Ash was old enough to be crawling and trying to pull up to stand. That would put him at about 8 months old. That is when he knocked over the bottle of chemical that scarred his hand.

        Of course, we don't know how far into the year 998, it is when Riley takes Ash to his adoptive parents. But, it is clear that she feared for his safety, in Valdor. She knew his birth family, and didn't trust that Luthor would take care of Ash. Possibly, Luthor didn't care about, or resented his little brother.
        Ash was 2 when Chronos stopped the meteors from falling. The current year, in the story, is 2014, making Ash at least 16 years old, but younger than 18 years. Chronos was a teenager in 998.
        Ash was not given up for adoption by his parents. He was an orphan, who could have ended up in Portia's orphanage.
        My question is, what was Riley's true motivation for pushing Ash off of the platform? She seemed to be in some sort of trance, and didn't remember doing it. Was she under Pedora's spell, though the dispel ring wasn't glowing, so that seems unlikely. Was she afraid for herself, if Chronos found out that she had hidden Ash, for all of those years? Or, was she afraid of Chronos recruiting Ash, or physically hurting him? He died as a result of being pushed off of the platform, did Riley know that his connection to the human world, would revive him?
        Riley's history and resulting motivations are the key to the mystery. I think she was trying to save his life, both because of Luthor and the disease that killed their parents. She could have also been afraid of what her father, the king, would do to Ash, considering Luthor's anger and rebellious nature. Maybe, the king wanted to exterminate everyone with human blood. Riley knew what her father was capable of, he killed her mother.

          Like the others have said, Ash wasn't placed for adoption, rather he was smuggled to a trustworthy family by Riley. So the question is, why did Riley feel the need to move Ash? Riley would have spent much time around the Timecrests. She obviously spent time with Luthor, but also with his parents as Chronos indicated she was close with his parents, especially after her mother succumbed to the virus. She was likely treated like part of the family, which means she would have loved Ash like family. When Julius and Helena died, murdered by Janus I believe as one of the flashbacks describes their dead bodies at his feet, Riley likely had first hand knowledge.

          1. Knowing Chronos' dislike for her father, she would have feared retaliation by Chronos, which means Chronos might impart his rage/hate for Janus on Ash. Knowing Julius wanted peace between Valdor and Humans, she wanted to get Ash as far away from Chronos as possible.
          2. Since Julius and Helena were gone, Janus would now likely target Chronos, which means Ash would be in danger, so she wanted to get Ash as far away from Chronos as possible.
          3. If Janus managed to defeat Chronos, Ash would have been in imminent danger, so she wanted to get him as far away from Janus as possible.

          3 reasons to move Ash and as ChristineMoon pointed out, what better place to hide him.
