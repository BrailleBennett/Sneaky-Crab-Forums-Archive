    [Darius Evenly]

    Anne explained her fire magic, and Erika explained her sword skills.

    I guess shouldn't be just lost in my thoughts this time. This is my chance to let them get to know me, so I need to do something.

    I pull back the string of my imbued bow.

    I whisper, Levitas.

    A swirling blue energy forms into a nocked, glowing arrow.

    Looking around for something aim at, I find my target, and release.

    The arrow flies from the bow and hits Erika. She gently begins to float a few feet into the air for a few seconds, before softly dropping back to the ground.

    I smile proudly for my demonstration for a moment--before realizing in horror that I might have scared Erika with my unexpected demonstration.

    And...

    I still haven't said anything. What's wrong with me?

    I cough, embarrassed, and say, "Shall we visit the Merchant? Aura potions might be of use in the upcoming trials?"

                          Dias sorry I don't want to seem rude. But if you could when you write try to express only your characters reaction. Like when you wrote about Darius not expecting the potion to be returned you wrote that he was surprised and had a shocked look. While Cowbunny may have had a different idea of how Darius reacted. Normally it would be a look of surprise I only mention it as you never know how a character may react and it could be different. Also try not to write outcomes out. You wrote that you did put the potion in his hand but Cowbunny may have wanted to write that he dropped it or I may have wrote that the potion slipped away and broke. Leaving the group a potion short and Darius 100 gold short. Perhaps causing Anne to have reated differently. If you do have a point where an outcome can arise please try to let that outcome play out. Again I don't want to be rude. I am just trying to make sure that everyone's characters react how the writer would like and how I as the narrator would like the story to play out. Situations like that often can create little plot twists for me and for the other writers. So please try to make sure you are writing for just your character. Thanks.

                            07-07-2016, 05:19 PM
                            Originally posted by Sashimioishi View Post
                            Dias sorry I don't want to seem rude. But if you could when you write try to express only your characters reaction.
                            I just wanted to step in and say let's make sure that this stays fun and positive! Sashimioishi is passionate about giving everyone control of their respective roles. But in collaborative storytelling, naturally everyone has lots of ideas on how to progress and it's fun to try to throw in little twists keep the story interesting.

                            I know that I've already broken some of sashimioishi's rules, like writing Erika's reactions to my arrow incident. But I think it goes to show it's also difficult since we're all very creative.

                            So here are the things we want to balance:
                            1. We want to give people full control of their respective roles (including the narrator)
                            2. We want to encourage creativity! The best part of writing is being creative and throwing in little surprises to delight the reader.
                            3. We all want to have fun!!

                            So I just want to say that giving people control is nice, because like Sashimioishi said, people may have specific intentions and feel bad if they don't have control of their own character. But creativity is important too. Every time someone has "broken" this rule, the spirit is good--they want to move the story along positively.

                            So let's move forward on this trying to follow the spirit of #1. But I think it's good if people push gently on the bounds with #2 to keep the story exciting. Think of it a little bit like improvisation acting--if you say YES-AND- when constructing a scene, people become better writers and better scenes come out. If someone pushes a bit too hard, you can just pull it back in your own text and just say you reacted a bit differently.

                            tldr: let's try to follow the rules, but if we push on it in the name of creativity, just gently bring it back where you want it, so everything stays positive, fun and creative!
