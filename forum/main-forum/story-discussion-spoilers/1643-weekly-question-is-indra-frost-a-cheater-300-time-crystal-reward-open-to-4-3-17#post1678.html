    Weekly Question: Is Indra Frost a cheater? 300 Time Crystal Reward! Open to 4-3-17!

    03-26-2017, 10:11 PM
    In Timecrest, we hear about the controversial Master of Water, Indra Frost. Many say that she has cheated, she skipped her trial, she may have used human technology, but in Chapter 9 we see her water tome put out all the fire at the Timecrest Library. This is the scene where questions are raised among the golds, and Roger's faith in her has been lost. But what about us? Who is she really? What are her motives? And will she appear more prominently in Timecrest 3, and if so, in what way? As with every weekly question, our first place winner is 300 Time Crystals, if we get 5 theories we will have a second prize of 100 Time Crystals, and if we have 8 theories we will have a random prize of 50 Time Crystals. So, without further ado, fire away!

      Before anything else, I'd say that Roger's faith was lost, not restored, when he realized Indra used human technology. He was disappointed over the foam, and stated that maybe he didn't believe in her, just wanted to believe in himself.

      That being said, I wouldn't call her a cheater, because there's no evidence that she cheated at anything.

      We know she used human tech. But just 15 years ago, humans were open to communicate with Alyncia (Ezra/ pocket watch, chapter 3), so human artifacts were likely acceptable. Heck- Chronos learned from time crystals, and no one would consider Alyncia's hero a cheater.

      Second, there is a huge difference in underhanded behavior and cheating. Look at Conner in the Copper Trials. No one will deny wanting to beat that little #%^* for what he did. But the rules were to survive, and show magical skill. He did both. Playing dirty, but not breaking rules or cheating. To call Indra a cheater would imply that she used an unnatural advantage against others competing for the same title of Water Master. But there's no text to imply this.

      Why did she not show up for her trials? Probably fear of being crucified for her human-assisted magic. Maybe she just felt that she didn't need to defend her actions.

      Why do I think she's a true water master? The scene in chapter 9, when she extinguished the fire magic. Not only does this show that she had the power to go up against a Fire Master without being present, the action saved the library. And a Master at heart would save the guild from accidents or evil.

      Only time will tell what the future brings, and what side any of the Masters will be on. I'm hoping she inspires Roger the way she did until chapter 9, and helps him gain the power to defeat Alan.

      When I first saw the scene at the library. I assumed she was a cheater, for using human technology, because that foam that was released from the tome seemed an awful lot like the foam from a fire extinguisher.

      But my theory changed because, as Christine mentioned, back when Indra was accused, we assume humans haven't been banished yet, and we know mages have pulled spells from many human relics before. The bomb in Chapter 8 is a perfect example of this. Another example to prove that most magic came from humans is the memory oracle. The first usage of magic was used to create a tree with red fruit. An apple tree. Then the first spell brings a human to Alyncia, which helps jumpstart the usage of magic.

      Back on topic, as far as we know, there wasn't, or wouldn't be, any sort of rule disallowing you to draw magic from human items back then, so as far as we know, Indra was still playing by the rules, her magic being quickly boosted by the usage of human objects, which any other Mage could've done.

      To prove that she truly was powerful, we look back to the same scene that started the whole topic, the library burning down in Chapter 9. The tome made by Indra that Roger was holding, shot out foam to stop the library from burning down. Even the Gold Mage who had been accusing her of cheating praised not only the power of the spell putting out the fire, but the fact Indra didn't even have to be there to cast it, and saw her in a different light.

      She didn't break any known laws, because they wouldn't have said you can't draw spells from human objects, humans weren't (presumably) banished at the time, and she cast a powerful spell without even being present. I say she's a legit Mage and probably one of the most powerful.

      I don't think Indra used human objects. I think she used something else.
      Everyone assumes that Indra used human technology to boost her powers, but there really is no human technology to do that. The only full-human mages were Alvin and possibly Julius. If only that many humans were able to cast magic, why invent a machine to help people cast magic? Unless humans wanted to help Alyncians improve their magic spells, they wouldn't do that. After the fall of Valdor, humans were chased out, killed, even their descendants had to hide. Unless they invented that machine before they were chased out, wouldn't every Alyncian before the fall of Valdor be using it? It just doesn't make sense.
      Furthermore, what human object can improve your magic other than Alvin's magic slate(seen in Ezra's memory oracles.)? It just gives Alvin what spells to use. Alyncians draw spells from objects, so unless if human objects can help Alyncians draw spells, there really is no item. Unless Indra was taking medicine or something, that would not be possible. Humans do not know how to wield aura. How would they know how to improve aura if they don't have any?
      I believe Indra's techniques involve the only thing that mages can improve their aura: aura potions. They can heal a mage's aura. Aura can be used to cast spells. If Indra was really strong, she would need strong aura. However, emerald dragons may be already extinct at the time, and only they can create the strongest aura potions. Synthetic ones can never reach that level. It would be completely possible if Indra stashed some real aura potions and used the lore of human technology to cover it up. Why did she not show up at the trial? It might be possible that that was the only way to escape death. If she had shown up, she would have to reveal that big secret that she was stashing real aura potions. She could not have them to herself to power herself anymore, and they might go to people in power.

      I dont believe she was greedy. I believe if she believed she had given them to the government, they might misuse them. King Janus Valdor is bloodthirsty, cruel, and overall a not good ruler. We dont even know what Chronos might be plotting.
      Since Indra was a thing of the past, her secret may never be revealed. The tome scene near the grand library may be that Indra had enchanted her tomes, so that she might help in the future some way to the people who believe in her. We will never know if Indra was a true cheater, or she did things for her own beliefs. Maybe she was greedy for her stash of aura potions, or she just wanted to hide them, but cant let them sit around with no use.
