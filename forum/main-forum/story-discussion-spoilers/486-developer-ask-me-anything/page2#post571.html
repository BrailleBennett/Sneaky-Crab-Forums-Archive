      Originally posted by Dias View Post
      Okey, here's another question. How does the guild get it's gold to pay to the mages and the rewards for the missions? maybe a Tax of sort, or maybe kind of alchemy?
      Great question. The Timecrest Guild is primarily funded through the mission system. When the citizens of Alyncia want the help of a mage, they register a mission with the Administration Chambers and put up the reward. The Guild takes a cut of the reward and pays out the rest to the mage or mages that complete the mission.

      Some of the missions can be dangerous, like investigating the Nightshade Forest. Some of them can be simple, like they need a water mage to help with a farmer's crops during a dry season.

      Most Alyncians hail Chronos as a hero who saved the world, and so to pay their respect, many also donate to the Timecrest Guild in support.

      But the mission system is the primary source of income for the guild.

                    Every time you complete Chapter 5 it saves it as a finished run, and should add a new row in the "Play Timecrest 2" button's dialog. Every time you finish chapter 10, it will save it as a finished run and add a new row in the "Play Timecrest 2" dialog, but you will not be able to continue from the completed Timecrest 2 games until the sequel is released.

                    You can only play one story at a time, but you can always start over to the beginning of Timecrest 1 or 2 if you don't mind losing your current progress. You will keep your gold, Time Crystals, Relationship points and special items (Like the Chronowand). It may take several play throughs to max our all of the relationship points in Timecrest 2.

                    If what I said is confusing please tell me, I can try to explain it better.
