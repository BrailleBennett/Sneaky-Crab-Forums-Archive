    Originally posted by Nyctofrost View Post
    What do all the characters look like?
    Most of the characters you have met so far look mostly human except for the Timecrest Merchant who is a Bunarf. There are other species that look different from humans in Alynica such as the Crystallis from Icecade and the Tenarans from Tenareign. Crystallis have clear crystal bodies and Tenarans are lizard people.

    Out of all the characters you have met, Guildmaster Fulgur is probably the most distinctively different from looking human because of his "hobby."

    We have gotten some sneak peaks as to what certain characters look like through Ash's eyes.


    The following link of Elise shows what she wears day-to-day. She typically hangs a charm from the chain of that dangles from her dress. Elise isn't wearing a cloak because she doesn't like the look of the Timecrest cloaks. This is quite surprising because we thought she liked black. Maybe she just doesn't like team spirit. Though she has been demoted now so she doesn't get a black cloak anymore.

    Elise: http://imgur.com/O6SCw45


    Ash takes more after Helena Timecrest, short, friendly and innocent looking with large purple eyes. Luthor takes more after Julius, tall, skinny, stoic and, in Ash's eyes, very pretentious. Though Luthor did also inherit his mother's eyes.

    Riley is taller than Ash and has long, wavy, brown hair that goes down to her waist. Her cheeks has a natural, light pink flush and she has sparkling blue eyes. Normally, she's very shy and doesn't speak up much, but when she does, her voice is always very warm. She has a Valdorian tattoo on her wrist.

    Valdorian Tattoo: http://imgur.com/H4OqulE


    We will eventually be releasing more character images along with their clothing, including official Timecrest cloaks. Though we will release these slowly because we want to spend time to nail down the looks of the characters in our images before we release them.

        #120
        07-07-2017, 06:40 PM
        Originally posted by Text View Post
        So if the time to write a chapter increases every chapter you write, can't you tie some paths together so 2 paths have the same ending? Or is it true that the decision you choose will be influenced by your earlier choices as well? (I have a hard time believing that for some reason)
        We built our own engine for Timecrest which allows us to do things like take decisions that you made at any point in the game and have them affect scenes later on in either small or drastic ways. For example, the story branches many more times after you choose Levitas or Gravitas, but your decision to choose either or will still be reflected in future story scenes. The same goes for if you got First Mage Ezra's training scene.

        When we started this, we felt that existing engines like Twine, while a really great resource for interactive fiction writers, won't give us all the tools we need to write the story we want.

        Because of our system and the ability to mix-and-match any choice with any path you take, there is no right or wrong path in Timecrest, just different experiences. While we do have converging paths, we try to avoid forcing any path into the same ending just for the sake of easier writing.

        You're right that each chapter takes longer and longer to write because of all the scenarios that we can cover. That is one of the biggest downsides to our system, but we are also excited at the prospect of telling a story on this grand of a scale.
