    Forum Contest: Feedback. WIN 100 TIME CRYSTALS! (6/19 to 6/26)

    06-19-2016, 05:25 PM
    Hello Timecresters and welcome to our eighth forum contest!

    Get your chance to win some free Time Crystals!

    This week's event will again be giving the best answer to the question below for a chance to win 100 Time Crystals!
    There will also be a participant randomly selected at the end of the event winning 50 Time Crystals!

    Rules and Information:
    - You may post multiple times but your entry will be only counted once.
    - Members are encouraged but not required to respond to other member's posts.
    - Posts must be relevant to the event or will not be counted as an entry.
    - Try to give the most thoughtful answer possible for the best chance to win.
    - We will direct PM your account if you are selected as a winner by the end of the contest.
    - Contest will end on June 26th, 2016.
    - Have fun!
    - Posts below may contain spoilers.

    To enter, please answer the following questions:

    1) Now that Timecrest 2: The Door is out, what do you like better about Timecrest 2 compared to Timecrest 1?
    2) What do you dislike about Timecrest 2 compared to Timecrest 1?
    3) What chapter are you currently playing?

    Good luck to all entries!

    Most likely my favorite part of Timecrest 2 compared to Timecrest 1 is how dynamic the story is. I played through Timecrest 1 at least 25 times - I'm not exaggerating, I have been playing over and over nonstop since early September - and I have always loved the story, however there were a limit to how many paths you could take, and a lot of times, when asked to make a decision, I had seen both of the outcomes come true, like Ash would answer first to your choice but then generalizes to the other choice, I hope that makes sense. But in the end, there were only so many paths to take, where you find Riley chained up on Aion, get taken out by Guildmaster Petora and the Hooded Man (Guildmaster Fulger) after refusing to take the king up on his offer (killing him to put him out of his misery) and leaving the palace, calling Ash a child after seeing his home destroyed, and he being frozen with guilt, and being killed by Guildmaster Petora at the top of Mount Asura, I believe are all of the alternate endings I discovered, however they all led back to the one central ending. Though I have not yet finished Timecrest 2, something I love about it is that every choice I make really matters, and I have to think hard about what advice to give Ash instead of knowing what will happen with every move. There are a ton of plot twists and turns that I didn't expect, most recently (Chapter 9 spoiler ahead)

    Spoiler:  
    when Ash is asked by Ezra to choose between saving the world or one person, and how Ezra tests his choice by killing Riley. Probably the hardest and most surprising thing was, after Ezra breaks Ash's neck and you turn back time, how you end up in Chapter 9 with Riley still dead. Out of all that I have experienced, that was the most shocking.


    But to sum it all up again, really my favorite thing about Timecrest 2 as compared to Timecrest 1 is how every choice you make matters and how drastically your choice effects Ash's life. Big shout-out to the developers for making an incredible continuation to the story!!! It was soooo worth the wait and I really appreciate all the hard work you guys put into it!!! Thanks for making this great game even greater!!!

          May contain spoilers, so you’ve been warned

          Well, that’s a tough one. I love Timecrest 1, but I must admit Timecrest 2 catched me way faster than I thought. In Timecrest 1, it was you and Ash against the world; you had to follow the clues and try to stop the meteors from falling once again over Alyncia. The mystery is there, sure, but in Timecrest 2, the mystery of the door, the messages directed to you, Ash’s dreams, not knowing what to think about Ash´s childhood hero, Sol Ignis, plus all the mysteries from Timecrest 1 makes the story so intriguing that you cannot help but wonder and imagine what could possibly be happening in Alyncia and what is your role there. How can you help? Craving the answer to all mysteries hidden inside this world.
          Also, I like it better that now you get to meet these amazing characters, which become these amazing friends with Ash and with you. I think this was the best of it. To have someone you can trust in the game where, before, you couldn’t trust anyone, it’s amazing. You really care for the characters to the point you want to make sure they stay safe during this huge trial that’s coming towards all of you.
          Lastly, I believe Timecrest 2 is much more concrete, if that makes sense. You see, in Timecrest 1, you were new to this world, you had no idea about the characters, and whom were you supposed to trust; everything was new and all the mysteries started to add up to the point where you just wondered about the answers. In this first game, you start caring for Ash.
          But, In Timecrest 2, you know Ash and you definitely care for him (I do). You are not flying as blindly anymore. You’ve meet Chronos and the rest of the characters, and you can make a better judgment about things that you know. The new mixed with the old, and you got a new world that’s familiar but it’s still hiding things. You are not a stranger anymore, you care for what is happening and will happen to your team, but still you don’t know everything. This is what makes the story so intriguing and really makes you feel like you are a parte of team 13.


          Well, certainly the choices in Timecrest 2 are tougher and they have so many consequences you don’t know what to do! It’s kind of love-hate with that because while it gives an amazing story, you are confused on what to do to save team 13. Also, I’m finding Timecrest 2 way more tragic than Timecrest 1. I think this is the lowest moment for Ash, which will make him rise with greater strength, but that doesn’t help with the truly sad parts of Timecrest 2.

          I’m currently on chapter 9, just after A.C. was killed (it's really really sad!)

          And lastly, I just want to say congratulations on this amazing game. I seriously believe it’s one of the best games I’ve ever played. I love how the player interacts with the characters, especially Ash. Thanks for all your effort; I can’t wait to see what’s next!

                  I haven't finished Timecrest 2 yet (currently in chapter 8) but I really enjoy how every choice has more impact on the story (can't just slack off on the choices and choose whatever now, haha) in comparison to the prequel and how each person has been developed further. I ended up choosing what choices I wanted and ended up not maxing out enough relationship points with Elise, A.C. or Roger so I decided it was better to go see Ezra (yes, finally learn more about him!) partly because I would not be able to see any special scenes, haha. It was great seeing Ash’s drawing skills which I have been curious about since the prequel. The Timecrest Merchant looked much different than the image in my mind.

                  Another great thing about Timecrest 2 is the amount of new information (Ezra spoilers) such as Ezra being the first King of Valdor? Just WHAT? Completely unexpected, Ezra being First Mage was predicted but a former King of Valdor?! And Chronos is still mysterious, especially in chapter 7 where there seems to be a connection between him and the player - do we know Chronos? Was he the one who gave the time changing power to us? Even Riley with her great powers, expertise and knowledge was surprised to hear it wasn't Ash who had the time changing powers. Also, the part where Chronos says ‘But I am not Luthor, anymore.’ raises even more questions such as: is the person known as Luthor Timecrest and Chronos different people? Did this change occur when he fought with Ezra? Chronos saying ‘Your brother’ rather than something along the lines of ‘As your brother’ also bugs me.

                  And Elise is also a mystery, it feels like there are more questions than answers – we learnt that Aion was indeed the Valdor palace and Ezra was the First Mage. But why did Ezra and Chronos fight? To the extent where meteors that could destroy the world were created? If Ash’s and Luthor’s dad created the palace, where is he now? How choices link events together is great too. Marik would remember if you helped him, Conner would attack you if you told Adam's mum about him - Conner seems to be perspicacious and I am hoping to learn more about him (he doesn’t seem to be a completely bad person – I feel like he may be the one who put Adam’s remains in an ornate box rather than an urn). I really enjoy the addition of music and scrolling in ‘History’ in Timecrest 2 and how the glitch regarding viewing information for items in the pouch was fixed.

                  It seems that maxing relationship points with Elise would be the most difficult, I don't even know if I will be able to get enough for A.C. or Roger in the next play-through. This is the only thing about Timecrest 2 that I find difficult to deal with, how to max out enough relationship points with everyone (it seems that the only option is to focus on maxing out only one person's gauge in each play-through). I don't see much use for Aura? It seems to be another way to use Gold up unless Aura plays an important part in the story (the only role I have seen is that you can't progress further with zero Aura)? I couldn't help but laugh learning A.C. called his sword Breakfast, hahahahaha. The increasingly complex world in Timecrest 2 makes it more fun than before to play. Seeing a use for Irillium was great, being able to use Magnus spells more was a nice addition (the hours learning a Magnus spell was not futile!) and the only thing I really want to see more is Morty (it’s fun imagining Morty growing up and joining Ash in his adventure although that’s pretty unlikely). Overall, I enjoy Timecrest 2 more than Timecrest 1 although the newly added relationship gauge requires more effort and thought when playing.

                      Just thought I would throw this out as suggested so others can see my feedback. Great game.

                      So far everything has been good. I had one bug where the story froze but I addressed that through support. I had no issues upgrading either.

                      The story certainly makes sense. And I believe whatever doesn't make sense will be clarified in Timecrest 3. I kind of feel that some of the wait times are a bit long and some of them I think you could make longer. Like in the end where Ash is seeing sections of his past there are several sections of four hour increments. I think some players might get discouraged seeing a four hour increment after two others. Perhaps keep the same amount of time but vary the pauses between. That part is a very exciting part of the story as you get to see more of Ash and Luthor and it allows you to speculate why Luthor attacked Valdor.

                      One section though that didn't make sense to me was the bomb section. I assume that the idea was to try and show that Ignis, Chronos, and his followers were using human technology as hinted when Chronos has the laptop. But later in the story it is written that the orders were not to kill Ash but this bomb seems almost left as a trap. I also saw that it was an opportunity to draw a spell but with the bomb killing Ash it seems to contradict the latter part of the story.

                      I really like the character development of Ezra as I still can't quite figure him out but I feel like I know him better at the same time.

                      Chronos has become a confusing character in a good way as it keeps me guessing on how to respond to him.

                        Too bad I missed this contest because I was trying to hold back on heavily reading these forums until I finished Timecrest 2, but there's always next time. Congrats to the winners!

                        I wanted to post my answer anyways because I'm truly impressed with what the developers have done with Timecrest 2. I absolutely love all the new characters you've added to the world. I agree with Dias that in in the first game you felt more alone because you just had Ash and Riley, but in the second game the world feels so vibrant and alive. I can imagine how difficult it must be to build such a massive world with complex characters and interesting plot and mysterious, but I felt this was done and done well in Timecrest 2. It's also satisfying that the second edition of the game answered the questions I had after playing through the first while introducing newer, enticing mysterious. I agree with Sashimioishi that most likely we'll get the same satisfaction in Timecrest 3 regarding the mysterious that now cross my mind after playing through the second one, although I have theories of my own.

                        So I liked Timecrest 1, but I loved Timecrest 2. Overall Timecrest 1 felt like a light introduction and it felt like the devs opened the door, or so to speak, with Timecrest 2.

                        In terms of what I enjoyed about Timecrest 1 over Timecrest 2, I did like that the first game was more lighthearted and eased people into the world. Timecrest 2 is much darker and pulls at my heart. I also did build a strong connection to Ash in the first game which put weight on a lot of my decisions in the second one.

                        I'm currently starting another playthrough of the game and I'm already learning new things I didn't during my first playthrough because of my choices. Timecrest 2 was worth the wait and really makes me excited of what is to come for this story.
